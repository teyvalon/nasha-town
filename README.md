TEYVALON is inspired by the classic board game [The Resistance: Avalon](https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/128882/the-resistance-avalon) by Don Eskridge.

## Get Started

TEYVALON is usually played with **5-8 players**. Smaller parties will be filled with bots so you will still play with 5 players.

## The Basics

All players are divided into 2 camps:

* The Nasha Townsfolk - defend the town and protect Columbina
* The Abyssals - ruin the Moon-Payer Night and hunt Columbina

Everyone's identity is secret. The Abyssals know each other but the townsfolk don't. Special roles know extra
information, see Roles below.

The number of players in each camp are as follows:

| Total Players | Nasha Town | Abyssals |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| 5 | 3 | 2 |
| 6 | 4 | 2 |
| 7 | 4 | 3 |
| 8 | 5 | 3 |

There will be 5 waves of Wild Hunt attacks. Everyone could use their Elemental Sight (or long-term Vision) skill
to see them behind the abyss mist. All players will be the **team leader** in turn to propose teams to place
bombs in the abyss mist to stop the Wild Hunts from ruining the Moon-Prayer Night. The number of Wild Hunts in
each wave of attack depends on the total number of players:

| Total Players | Wave 1 | Wave 2 | Wave 3 | Wave 4 | Wave 5 |
|---------------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 5 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 3 | 3 |
| 6 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 4 |
| 7 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 4* | 4 |
| 8 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 5* | 5 |

For each wave of the attacks, the leader in turn should propose a team of the same size as the incoming Wild Hunts.
After proposal, everyone votes for or against that team. Only when **more than half** of the players approve, can a
team get approved. Once approved, each of the team member will get a bomb to place in the abyss mist. Otherwise if
a team is rejected, the next leader in turn shall propose a new team, and everyone votes again. The total number of
proposals per wave of attack is 3 by default, and it is configurable inside the hut before the game starts. If all
teams are rejected, the Abyssals win immediately and the game ends.

Each bomb kills exactly one Wild Hunt. The Abyssal team member may replace the bomb with a fake one, while the
Townsfolk can only place real bombs. Fake bombs won't kill Wild Hunt, so Wild Hunts will pass the abyss mist. There
are 2 guards sent by Capitano to help protect the Nasha Town. If any Wild Hunt passes the abyss mist, the guards
will eliminate them, at the cost that one guard will get infected by the Abyss and leave for rest.

If all guards are gone and there are still Wild Hunts coming into the town, the Abyssals win the game. Or else if
the remaining waves of Wild Hunts is insufficient to ruin the Moon-Prayer Night, the Nasha Town wins the game (see
also Roles for when Rerir is in game). This is basically equivalent to the 5 rounds of missions in The Resistance,
and the best 3 out of 5 wins the game.

`*` For games with 7 or more players, Capitano will send extra reinforcement in Wave 4 only, when the Abyssals will
have to let 2 or more Wild Hunts in to score for that round. The reinforcement will retire after Wave 4
regardlessly.

## Roles

There are currently 3 game modes to select:

1. **Basic** - no special roles, it's basically the original The Resistance game
2. **Prophecy** - Columbina the real "Moon Goddess" in Nasha Town, Rerir the "Moon Hunter" in the Abyssals
3. **Veil** - Columbina and Lauma the "Moonchanter" in Nasha Town, Rerir and Dottore the "False Moon" in the Abyssals

### Rerir and Columbina

If the Nasha Town scores 3 points first, the game doesn't end when Rerir is in the Abyssals. Rerir will get an
extra chance to identify who is the real Columbina among all the Townsfolk (the Moon-Hunting). If Rerir found the
real Columbina, the Abyssals win the game.

Columbina knows all the Abyssal players at the beginning of the game. But she must hide her own identity because
of the existance of Rerir. So Columbina should play smart to both lead the Nasha Town camp to win the game, and
avoid being too straightforward to draw Rerir's attention.

### Dottore and Lauma

At the beginning of the game, Lauma sees two players with the Moon's power, but she doesn't know exactly which is
Columbina and which is Dottore. Meanwhile, Dottore shall play as if he is the real Moon Goddess to mislead the
Townsfolk and bring victory to the Abyssals. Therefore, Lauma must use her wisedom to figure out who is the real
Moon Goddess and lead the team to protect the Nasha Town.
